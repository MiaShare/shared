Two Issues Investigated

IssueRoot CauseVerdict
"No payment method on file" Tool used applicant_id FK to find payment methods. 76.3% (242/317) of Molloy's active payment methods have applicant_id = NULL — they're linked only via user_id. BUG — Fixed in PR #5557
"No upcoming payments found" Molloy's standard plan is $3,500 across 4 monthly installments of $875. All Molloy students with exactly 4 completed $875 payments have is_active=0, is_satisfied=1 — the plan was genuinely complete. LEGITIMATE — Not a bug

Scope of Impact

At Molloy University, 242 out of 317 active payment methods (76.3%) have applicant_id = NULL, affecting 128 unique students. Every one of these students would have been told "No payment method on file" by the AI assistant, regardless of their actual payment status.

Solution: Switch to PaymentBrokerInterface Lookup

Fix

Root cause: PaymentScheduleTool eager-loaded paymentMethods via the Applicant model's relationship, which joins on applicant_id. But Molloy's Finix-based payment methods are linked only via user_id (with applicant_id = NULL). The student dashboard, payment forms, and autopay all use PaymentBrokerInterface::getPaymentMethodOptionsForUser() which queries by user_id — the canonical lookup. ROOT CAUSE

Fix: Switched from applicant-based eager loading to PaymentBrokerInterface::getPaymentMethodOptionsForUser($user, $institution). Also selects the default payment method (via is_default) rather than the newest by date. 2 FILES, +61/-59 LINES

Why the old code failed: Applicant::paymentMethods() queries payment_methods.applicant_id = applicant.id. When applicant_id is NULL (as it is for 76.3% of Molloy's Finix-based methods), the relationship returns zero results. The PaymentBrokerInterface uses user_id instead — the same lookup used by the student dashboard and payment forms.

Why "No Upcoming Payments" Was Legitimate

The original reporter's "No upcoming payments found" was correct behavior. Molloy's standard plan is $3,500 across 4 monthly installments of $875. Database analysis of Molloy students with exactly 4 completed $875 payments shows they all have is_active=0, is_satisfied=1 — their plans are genuinely complete with zero remaining periods. The $901.25 first payment = $875 base + $26.25 late fee (3% of $875). NOT A BUG
